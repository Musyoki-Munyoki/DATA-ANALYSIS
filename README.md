##### Customer Churn Analysis Report
##### 1. Introduction

Customer retention is a critical challenge for telecommunications companies, as acquiring new customers is often more expensive than retaining existing ones. 
This analysis was conducted to examine patterns in customer behavior and identify factors associated with customer churn. By understanding these patterns, the company can develop strategies to reduce churn and improve customer retention.

The dataset analyzed contains 7,043 customers and 21 variables, including demographic information, services subscribed to, billing information, and whether the customer churned.

##### 2. Data Preparation

Before conducting the analysis, the dataset was cleaned to ensure accuracy and consistency.

Key preparation steps included:

Converting the TotalCharges column from text format to a numeric format.

Handling missing values that appeared during the conversion.

Removing rows with incomplete data to maintain the integrity of the analysis.

After cleaning, the dataset was ready for exploratory data analysis.

##### 3. Key Findings
##### 3.1 Overall Customer Churn

The analysis shows that a significant portion of customers have discontinued their services. While the majority of customers remain with the company, the churn rate is high enough to warrant attention and strategic intervention.

##### 3.2 Contract Type and Churn

Contract type was one of the strongest indicators of customer churn.

Customers with month-to-month contracts exhibited the highest churn rate. In contrast, customers with one-year and two-year contracts were significantly more likely to remain with the company.

This suggests that longer contractual commitments increase customer retention.

##### 3.3 Tenure and Customer Loyalty

Tenure analysis revealed that customers with shorter tenures were much more likely to churn.

Many customers who left the company did so within the early stages of their subscription, indicating that the first few months of the customer lifecycle are critical for retention.

Customers who remained with the company for longer periods were significantly less likely to churn.

##### 3.4 Monthly Charges and Churn

Customers who churned tended to have higher average monthly charges compared to those who stayed.

This finding suggests that pricing, perceived value, or dissatisfaction with service costs may influence the decision to leave.

##### 3.5 Internet Service Type

Customers using fiber optic internet services showed a noticeably higher churn rate compared to DSL users and customers without internet services.

This could indicate potential concerns related to pricing, service reliability, or customer expectations regarding high-speed internet services.

##### 3.6 Payment Method

Payment method also appeared to influence churn behavior.

Customers using electronic checks had the highest churn rate, while customers using automatic payment methods such as bank transfers and credit cards were more likely to remain with the company.

Automatic billing may therefore encourage longer customer relationships.

##### 4. Business Implications

The analysis highlights several actionable insights that the company can use to reduce customer churn:

Customers with month-to-month contracts represent the highest churn risk.

New customers are more likely to churn during the early stages of their subscription.

Higher monthly charges may contribute to customer dissatisfaction.

Fiber optic users show elevated churn rates, suggesting possible service or pricing issues.

Customers using manual payment methods are less loyal than those using automated billing.

##### 5. Recommendations

Based on the findings, the following strategies are recommended:

Encourage customers to move from month-to-month contracts to longer-term contracts through incentives and discounts.

Implement customer onboarding and engagement programs during the first few months of service.

Review pricing strategies, particularly for high monthly charge plans.

Investigate potential issues affecting fiber optic service customers.

Promote automatic payment methods to improve customer retention.

##### 6. Conclusion

This analysis provided valuable insights into the factors influencing customer churn within the telecom company. Contract type, tenure, monthly charges, internet service type, and payment methods were all found to significantly influence customer retention.

By addressing these factors through targeted strategies, the company can reduce churn rates, improve customer satisfaction, and ultimately increase long-term revenue.
